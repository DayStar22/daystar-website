PAGE 4 OF 4 — RECOMMENDATIONS & ASSESSOR NOTE

Top 3 Ranked Recommendations

Ranked by estimated impact on overall score and final yield quality.

  • #1

    Tighten VPD to the 1.2–1.5 kPa Window for Late Flower

    Your current RH of 52% at 77°F places VPD at approximately 1.62 kPa — above the optimal late-flower range. Dropping RH to 45–48% or reducing temp by 2°F will reduce mold risk and improve resin production in the final two weeks.

    Estimated score impact: +4–5 pts

  • #2

    Implement Runoff EC Monitoring & Adjust Feed Accordingly

    No runoff EC tracking was reported. In coco/perlite at week 6 flower, buildup is predictable. Measure runoff EC at every watering and adjust feed strength to keep runoff within 10–15% of input EC.

    Estimated score impact: +3–4 pts

  • #3

    Establish a Trichome-Based Harvest Protocol Before Week 8

    Harvest timing by calendar week alone leaves yield and potency on the table. Acquire a jeweler's loupe (60–100x) and begin trichome checks at day 49. Target 10–20% amber trichomes for this cultivar's optimal harvest window.

    Estimated score impact: +3 pts

A Note from Lance

This is a solid grow. Your lighting investment is paying off — the LM301H spectrum is well-matched to Wedding Cake's needs, and your genetics sourcing reflects real diligence. The gaps here are procedural, not structural. You don't need to rebuild your setup; you need to add two monitoring habits and dial in your late-flower environment. Do those three things and you're looking at an 82–86 on your next assessment. That's the difference between a good run and a run you're proud to show someone. — Lance Fisher, DayStar Consulting
